# CPA Ethics CPE Requirements by State

> **CPETrack Reference** — Full state details at [cpe.paprika-labs.app](https://cpe.paprika-labs.app)

Ethics CPE is one of the most confusing areas of CPA licensing because every state has different:
- Total hours required per cycle
- Whether a state-specific ethics course is mandatory
- Which providers are approved
- Whether ethics counts toward the general CPE total or is separate

## States with NO Ethics CPE Requirement
- **Georgia** — no ethics mandate (unique among states)
- **South Dakota** — ethics required only for initial licensure (CPA exam), not for renewal

## States with State-Specific Ethics Courses Required
These states require a course covering their specific statutes/rules, not just generic ethics:
California, Florida, Indiana, Louisiana, Mississippi (partial), Ohio, Pennsylvania (partial), Tennessee, Texas

## Common Ethics CPE Mistakes

1. **Confusing per-cycle vs per-year** — some states require ethics annually, others per renewal cycle
2. **Using unapproved providers** — always check if your state requires state-board-approved ethics only
3. **State-specific confusion** — taking a generic NASBA ethics course when a state-specific one is required
4. **Louisiana biennial trap** — ethics only required in even-year renewals; no requirement in odd years
